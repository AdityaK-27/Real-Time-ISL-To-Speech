# Model Training Cheat Sheet

Here is an easy-to-understand breakdown of every single experiment we ran. This explains exactly what makes each model unique and how it handled the data.

---

## 🟢 Category 1: The Original Small Dataset (ST-GCN)
These models were trained exclusively on the original dataset (~21 videos per class). The architecture is PyTorch's **ST-GCN** (Spatial-Temporal Graph Convolutional Network), which mathematically treats the body as a 3D physical skeleton.

* **`baseline`** 
  * **What it is:** The control group. 
  * **How it trained:** Takes the raw, jittery (`60x201`) keypoints exactly as MediaPipe outputs them and feeds them straight into the model. No smoothing, no trickery.
* **`aug_only`** 
  * **What it is:** The Randomizer.
  * **How it trained:** Uses **Dynamic Augmentation**. Every time the model looks at a frame during training, it subtly shifts, scales, or rotates the skeleton slightly on-the-fly. This was meant to teach the model how to handle different angles but artificially broke the physical constraints of the skeleton.
* **`kalman_only`** 
  * **What it is:** The Smoother.
  * **How it trained:** Uses a mathematical **Kalman Filter** with highly tuned parameters (`Q=1e-2`). It acts like a shock-absorber on the raw data, perfectly erasing the shaky jitter caused by the webcam *before* the model ever sees the data.
* **`full_pipeline`** 
  * **What it is:** The Kitchen Sink.
  * **How it trained:** Applies both the **Kalman Filter** perfectly smoothing the data, AND the **Dynamic Augmentation** shifting it around randomly. 

---

## 🟡 Category 2: Physical Augmentation (ST-GCN)
These models also use the small dataset, but handle data expansion differently. Instead of randomizing frames on the fly, they mathematically generate permanent, physically altered videos on the hard drive to inflate the dataset size.

* **`offline_aug`** 
  * **What it is:** The Inflator.
  * **How it trained:** Uses **Offline Augmentation**. We explicitly split the dataset so the test data was safe, then multiplied the training data by forcing "left-handed mirroring" and "slow-motion time-warping". It essentially took 15 training videos and artificially created 75 training videos.
* **`kalman_off_aug`** 
  * **What it is:** Smoothed Inflator.
  * **How it trained:** It first applies the **Kalman Filter** shock-absorber to the original videos, and *then* multiplies them 5X via **Offline Augmentation**.

---

## 🔵 Category 3: The User-Recorded Large Dataset (ST-GCN)
These models represent the true evolution of the project. They ditch all artificial augmentation tricks and instead use **Real Data** (merging the original videos with the new videos you personally recorded on your webcam). Total size: ~31 videos per class.

* **`large_raw`** 🏆 *(The Champion Model - 95.2%)*
  * **What it is:** The Purist.
  * **How it trained:** Feeds the massive combined raw dataset straight into the ST-GCN. It proves that deep learning models naturally ignore camera noise when you give them enough raw human variance to learn from. 
* **`large_kalman`** 
  * **What it is:** The Over-Smoother.
  * **How it trained:** Applies the **Kalman Filter** to the massive combined dataset. Because the model already had enough data to be smart, mathematically smoothing the data accidentally erased tiny micro-movements of your fingers, heavily confirming the paradox in your research paper.

---

## 🟣 Category 4: The Legacy Architecture Comparison
These models use an entirely different mathematical engine to prove that Graph Networks (ST-GCN) are superior for skeleton data.

* **`lstm_baseline` / `lstm_large_raw`**
  * **What it is:** The Sequence Learner.
  * **How it trained:** Rather than tracking interconnected bones in 3D space like a graph, this model uses a TensorFlow **LSTMv3** architecture. It treats the 201 coordinates as a massive, flat sequence of numbers (like reading a paragraph of text) completely ignoring the physical bone connections of the human body.
